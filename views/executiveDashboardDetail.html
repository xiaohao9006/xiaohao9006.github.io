WHAT WAS I ASKED TO DO

Design a KPI dashboard to provide Agency Executives the capability to quickly assess the current health of their business performance

Enable them to view positive and negative deviations, obtain clarification on those deviations, allowing them to react accordingly

DESIGN PROCESS

EMPATHIZE
DEFINE
IDEATE
PROTOTYPE
TEST

Develop a deep customer empathy. Understand the customer's needs and desires.

"Design Partnership" Program

During the empathize phase, I, together with PM and developer lead, just jumped on the “Design Partner” call, and asked those Agency Executives about their data analytics practices and requirements:

What data do you think is important to you?
What insights do you want to get from the data?
How do you want to analyze and compare the data?
What are your current data analytics practices? Any pain points?
...
"Design Partnership" Program

A target group of users are invited to a weekly “focus group” session so that we can talk with them and validate our product concepts, vision and roadmaps, and also do usability testing and gather feedback.

Provides focus and frames the problem. Fuels brainstorming "how might we statements".

Persona
WALTER
Age: 46
Agency Size: A1
Role: Executive Memeber
Line of Business: P&C, Benefits Mix

Based on the findings from previous phase, we defined target persona as "WALTER". He is an executive member and responsible for the agency as a whole and he has access to view all financial information for the agency.

All he wants is to "Identify Deviation" - to look for "illnesses" and where they are occurring through the data, so that he/she can take quick actions. But the current data analytics practices are heavily "hard copy report based", which is not real-time and is hard to gain insights.

How Might We Statements
Create ...

A fully engaging and easy to understand business performance dashboard

For ...

Agency executives

To ...

Help them easily "identify deviation" and take quick actions in real time

And improve ...

Their overall in house data analytics process and practices

Explore as many ideas as possible that serve those customer needs.

Whiteboard Brainstorming

Team of 3 (PM, dev lead and I) constantly got together and brainstormed ideas. White board was our powerful tool.

Create & show a physical form to your solution prototype - anything: paper, interactive.

Wireframe Examples

I brought back brainstorming ideas and white board sketches to create detail wireframes and prototypes.

Rapid Prototyping in Jsfiddle

And in order to rapidly test out data visualization ideas, I also used jsfiddle sandbox to customize Highchart JS and tried out different design solutions.

Customize Highcharts JS

The final design solution we went for required lots of highcharts customization. The sandbox environment not only empowered us to explore different design ideas, but also gave our development team clue on how to implement the complex customization.

Test + collect feedback through use of rapid interaction cycles. Refine the solution. Measure & repeat.

Test with Design Partners

And again, every week PM, dev lead and I jumped on the "Design Partner" call and validated the design solutions with our customers. Based on the feedback, we continued to refine the solutions and do rapid iteration cycles.
